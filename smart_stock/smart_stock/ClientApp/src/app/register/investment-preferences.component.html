Choose Your Preferences

Blue Chip Trading
Based on low risk and slow, steady returns, blue chip trading is best for beginner investors who want to take an incremental, hands-off approach to trading. This strategy focuses on well known fortune 500 tickers, and stability above all else.
Long Term Trading
Similar to blue chip trading, but places a more intense focus on long term viability and growth potential.
Swing Trading
Higher risk trading strategy with higher potential returns. Focuses on more volatile tickers, and will execute several buy/sell cycles over the course of a week. Focuses on volume and momentum over stability and long term returns.
Scalp Trading

Similar to swing trading in the respect that this strategy focuses on momentum and volume, but takes more advantage of intra-day trading. As a result, this strategy is a mix between day trading and long trading, but tends to favor day trading. Make sure you have the funds for this!

Day Trading

Highest risk with highest potential returns. This strategy focuses exlcusively on intra-day trading, and will never hold over night. This selection will make more then three, but rather several (30++) buy/sell cycles throughout a single day, and as a result, your account net worth must exceed $25,000 before selecting this strategy.
